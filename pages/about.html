What I actually do

I'm a Computer Science undergrad at Vijaybhoomi University, majoring in AI. Right now I'm interning at ArcelorMittal Nippon Steel India, building a computer-vision safety monitoring system from the ground up — model training, backend, frontend, deployment, all of it. Outside that, I build things I'm personally curious about: an LLM-powered finance advisor, an ML pipeline for a problem on my own campus, a portfolio rebalancing engine. Five shipped systems so far, each one teaching me a different part of the stack.

How I work

I'd rather have one model at 97% accuracy that I understand completely than five I copy-pasted. When something doesn't work — and a lot doesn't, the first time — I want to know exactly why before I move on. That's slower. It's also the only way I've found to actually get good at something instead of just finishing it.
